1. Cloudflare WAF (The Shield) Its Job: Security gatekeeper.
Why it matters: Sites like guns.lol are constantly targeted by rival sites or bots trying to scrape rare usernames. Cloudflare blocks them before they ever touch your servers.

2. Vercel (The Presentation) Its Job: Hosts your frontend website design. Why it matters: It copies your code to servers all around the world. No matter where a visitor lives, your user profile pages snap open instantly.

3. Railway (The Brain) Its Job: Runs your backend server code. Why it matters: It handles the complex logic (like registering accounts, checking if a username is taken, and processing settings). It automatically handles spikes in traffic so your servers never freeze.

4. Drizzle ORM (The Free Bridge) Its Job: Connects your Railway backend code to your database. Why it matters: It is 100% free and lets you talk to your database in clean, modern code instead of writing long, messy SQL commands manually.

5. Upstash Redis (The Instant Memory) Its Job: Remembers profile text configuration in lightning-fast RAM. Why it matters: Instead of asking your database to dig up a user's bio information every single second, Upstash serves it from memory in under 2 milliseconds. This stops your database from getting overwhelmed.

6. Cloudflare R2 (The Media Vault) Its Job: Stores all profile pictures, custom background GIFs, and music. Why it matters: Standard servers charge massive fees for bandwidth. Cloudflare R2 has zero egress fees, meaning you can stream a heavy background GIF to millions of viewers for free. You only pay for the raw storage space used.

7. Supabase (The Vault) Its Job: Houses your primary user database. Why it matters: It is your single source of truth. It securely encrypts passwords, holds account IDs, tracks who paid for what, and remembers all text links.

8. Amazon SES (The Mailman) Its Job: Sends verification codes and password reset emails. Why it matters: It is the cheapest option on earth ($0.10 for every 1,000 emails). This keeps your bills tiny when you have millions of users registering accounts.

9. PayPal (The Cash Register) Its Job: Processes payments for premium upgrades. Why it matters: Since you dropped custom domains, you will make money by selling profile cosmetics (glowing text, premium profile badges, profile animations). Users buy these perks directly via a trusted PayPal button.
